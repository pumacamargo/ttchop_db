# AI UGC BLOCKBUSTER CHAOS TEMPLATE

## PURPOSE

Generate a **15-second hyperrealistic AI UGC video** designed to maximize retention, rewatches, comments, and product interest.

The creator is always the main character.

The product naturally supports the story but never becomes the primary focus.

The video should feel like a real creator accidentally running through multiple blockbuster movies in one continuous adventure.

---

# VIDEO LENGTH

**15 seconds total**

The video is divided into **three continuous cinematic scenes of approximately five seconds each.**

Every second should contain meaningful visual action.

No dead time.

No introductions.

---

# VISUAL STYLE

The entire video must be completely **hyperrealistic**.

It should be indistinguishable from footage captured with a professional cinema camera.

Everything should look physically believable:

* realistic lighting
* realistic explosions
* cinematic motion blur
* believable creatures
* authentic environments
* natural facial expressions
* realistic physics

The viewer should initially question whether the footage is real.

---

# VIDEO STRUCTURE

## SCENE 1 (0–5s)

### The Hook

The video begins immediately.

No greeting.

No introduction.

No explanation.

The creator is already running through an epic action sequence.

Examples:

* escaping dinosaurs
* medieval battlefield
* alien invasion
* giant robots
* collapsing city
* zombie outbreak
* massive animal stampede
* pirate battle
* fantasy war
* space battle

During the run, the creator says only one short sentence:

> "Only 1% of people watching this video will answer the last question correctly."

The creator never stops running.

The action continues around them.

---

## SCENE 2 (5–10s)

### Product Integration

The creator transitions directly into another blockbuster scene.

The new environment should be completely different from the first.

While continuing to run, the creator naturally introduces the product.

Example dialogue:

> "Good thing I brought my ____."

or

> "This is exactly why I always carry ____."

or

> "I would've been in trouble without ____."

The dialogue should feel completely natural.

The product should briefly appear on camera.

The creator continues moving while talking.

---

## SCENE 3 (10–15s)

### Final Challenge

The creator enters one final, even bigger action scene.

This should be the most visually spectacular moment.

The creator finishes with a question based entirely on something that actually appeared earlier in the video.

The answer must be clearly present within the video.

It should simply be difficult to notice on the first viewing.

Examples:

* "How many dinosaurs actually appeared?"
* "What color was the dragon?"
* "Which animal crossed behind me?"
* "How many UFOs flew overhead?"
* "What object disappeared?"
* "Which character appeared twice?"

The question should encourage viewers to immediately replay the video.

---

# BLOCKBUSTER WORLDS

Each scene should feel like a completely different blockbuster movie.

Possible worlds include:

* Medieval fantasy war
* Alien invasion
* Dinosaur attack
* Giant robots
* Cyberpunk city
* Zombie apocalypse
* Pirate battle
* Underwater kingdom
* Frozen kingdom
* Jungle adventure
* Haunted castle
* Giant monster attack
* Space battle
* Toy world
* Formula racing
* Street racing
* Ancient civilization
* Magical kingdom
* Post-apocalyptic wasteland

---

# EASTER EGGS

Hide several subtle visual details throughout the video.

Examples include:

* hidden animals
* duplicated characters
* recurring props
* changing clothing
* hidden symbols
* background jokes
* objects moving
* impossible continuity changes

These should support the final question.

The answer must always be visible somewhere in the video.

Never ask a question whose answer cannot be determined from the footage.

---

# PRODUCT INTEGRATION

The product should never interrupt the story.

It should feel like a natural part of the creator's adventure.

The adventure remains the main attraction.

The product simply becomes part of the creator's survival or journey.

---

# CAMERA STYLE

The video should feel like a continuous handheld shot filmed by another person running beside the creator.

Use:

* realistic handheld movement
* cinematic framing
* dynamic camera motion
* natural focus changes
* realistic lens behavior
* believable camera shake

The creator should remain visible during almost the entire video.

---

# DIALOGUE STYLE

Dialogue should be extremely concise.

Keep each spoken line under one sentence.

The visuals should tell most of the story.

The creator sounds calm, authentic, and conversational.

Never sound like an advertisement.

---

# FINAL GOAL

Create a **15-second hyperrealistic AI UGC video** where viewers stay for the nonstop blockbuster action, become curious about the product naturally, and immediately replay the video to answer the final observation question. Every replay should reveal details they missed the first time.
