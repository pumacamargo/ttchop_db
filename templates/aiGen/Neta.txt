This video takes place inside a completely normal home environment such as a living room, kitchen, hallway, bedroom, or workspace. The setting should feel naturally lived in with ordinary household objects visible. Nothing should appear staged or professionally prepared.

VISUAL PLAN

The visual plan must be written entirely in English.

The dialogue must never appear inside the visual plan.

Describe only visible actions and camera behavior.

Describe:

• imperfect handheld smartphone recording
• natural home lighting
• normal household environment
• casual creator movements
• continuous recording with no cinematic camera work
• the product being used naturally as part of one simple demonstration

The creator should never appear to "present" the product.

Instead, the creator simply performs a short action using the product while casually talking.

The product itself becomes the center of attention because of what it is doing, not because the creator is describing it.

The visual progression should follow this structure:

1. The creator casually starts recording while already holding or standing near the product.

2. Without introducing it dramatically, they begin one simple demonstration or interaction that creates immediate visual curiosity.

3. The camera naturally follows the demonstration while remaining handheld and slightly imperfect.

4. The creator briefly points at or touches part of the product while continuing the demonstration.

5. The demonstration finishes naturally, with the creator casually lowering the product or stepping back as the recording ends.

There should be no dramatic reveals, no cinematic transitions, no exaggerated reactions, and no staged acting.

Everything should feel like someone casually showing something interesting they happen to have.

DIALOGUE

The dialogue must be written entirely in VIDEO LANGUAGE.

The creator speaks exactly like someone sending a quick message to a friend.

Structure:

1. Begin by casually mentioning what they're doing right now.

2. Briefly explain the simple thing they like about this product while the demonstration is happening.

3. Finish by casually mentioning that if someone wants to see how much it costs, the link is right there below.

The language must use extremely simple vocabulary.

The creator should sound:

• relaxed
• slightly distracted by what they're doing
• conversational
• authentic
• completely unscripted

Avoid:

• hooks
• storytelling
• marketing language
• feature lists
• specifications
• product claims
• exaggerated excitement
• urgency
• direct selling language

The dialogue should always feel secondary to the visual demonstration.
The visuals create the curiosity.
The dialogue simply accompanies what the viewer is already watching.
